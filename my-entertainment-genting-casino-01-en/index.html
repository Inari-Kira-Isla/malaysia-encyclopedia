Genting Highlands: The Legendary Story of Malaysia's Only Legal Casino

The Founding of Genting Highlands: The Legend of Lim Goh Tong

In 1963, when Lim Goh Tong, then 55 years old, first set foot on the barren mountain plateau of Pahang's Genting Highlands, he already had an ambitious vision in mind: to transform this 1,800-meter-above-sea-level plateau into Southeast Asia's premier integrated leisure resort destination. Born in China's Fujian province and having migrated to Malaysia in the 1930s, this entrepreneur spent nearly a decade overcoming funding shortfalls, technical challenges, and skeptical voices before successfully securing Malaysia's sole legal casino license from the government in 1971, with operations officially commencing that same year.（Data Source: Genting Group Official Historical Records）

Lim Goh Tong's entrepreneurial journey was fraught with hardships. During the initial construction phase, building materials had to be transported piece by piece from the foothills to the plateau, as adequate road infrastructure was lacking—a challenge beyond imagination. What proved even more difficult was that Malaysia's economy in the 1960s had not yet taken off, and investors generally adopted a wait-and-see attitude toward this seemingly audacious project.It is estimated that Lim Goh Tong invested over 200 million ringgit of his own funds in the project's early stages, once facing a severe financial crisis.He once recalled: "If this project fails, I will lose everything, but I believe humanity's demand for leisure and entertainment will continue to grow." This unwavering conviction ultimately paid off. Today, Genting Highlands welcomes over 20 million visitors annually, becoming a vital pillar of Malaysia's tourism industry.（Data Source: Tourism Malaysia 2023 Statistics）

Lim Goh Tong is respectfully referred to as the "Father of the Leisure Industry" in the sector, having proven the feasibility of developing a mountain resort in a tropical nation. His success lay not in luck, but in his precise judgment of market trends and relentless execution. Today's Genting Highlands has evolved into a diversified industrial cluster encompassing gaming, hotels, theme parks, and convention centers—a business model that has been referenced and adopted by multiple Asian countries.

  • Travel Tips:For first-time visitors to Genting Highlands, it is recommended to reserve at least two days and one night accommodation to fully experience the highland's natural environment and indoor entertainment facilities.

Lim Goh Tong's entrepreneurial story demonstrates the power of vision and execution.Genting Highlands' success underscores the importance of "differentiated positioning"—in the highly competitive Southeast Asian tourism market, choosing a unique entry point and making sustained investments is key to establishing long-term competitive advantage.This remains valuable insight for modern entrepreneurs. Leveraging mountain resources as a foundation combined with the scarcity of gaming licenses, Lim Goh Tong created an irreplicable business moat—a strategic approach worth learning from across industries.

Genting Highlands: Malaysia's Only Legal Gambling Destination

Pursuant to the Malaysian Gaming Laws 1965 Amendment, only one legal casino is permitted to operate nationwide, making Resorts World Genting at Genting Highlands the sole exception. In 2023, Genting Highlands welcomed over 23 million visitors throughout the year, with the casino area averaging approximately 15,000 to 20,000 daily entries and exits—these figures amply demonstrate its dominant position within Malaysia's gaming industry.

Genting Highlands' casino covers approximately 200,000 square feet (about 18,580 square meters), featuring over 3,000 slot machines and more than 500 traditional gaming tables including baccarat, blackjack, and roulette. The casino operates 24 hours a day, and during the Lunar New Year period each year, a VIP high-stakes area is established where single bets can reach up to 50,000 Ringgit (approximately $10,700 USD). The premises include several premium VIP rooms equipped with dedicated dealers and personal services, available for high-tier clients with每一次投注額 exceeding 1,000 Ringgit per wager.

  • Galaxy Building: Equipped with over 1,200 of the latest model slot machines, including internationally renowned brands such as MGM Grand and Aristocrat
  • SkyAvenue: Opened in 2018, offers an integrated gaming and entertainment experience, featuring themed restaurants and performance venues
  • VIP Clubhouse: Invitation-only members only, managed through a membership system, providing personalized gaming services and exclusive concierge assistance

It is noteworthy that Malaysian law explicitly prohibits local Muslims from entering the casino, a regulation stemming from Islamic Sharia law's strict prohibitions against gambling. Pursuant to the 1994 Gaming Tax Act, Genting Highlands is required to pay approximately 25% of its gaming revenue to the government—in 2023, this is estimated to have contributed over 1.2 billion Ringgit (approximately $256 million USD) in tax revenue to the national treasury, making it a significant source of national income.

Non-Gaming Entertainment: Genting's Diversification Strategy

According to Resorts World Genting's 2023 Annual Report, non-gaming revenue has accounted for 43% of total revenue, a significant increase from 31% in 2018, reflecting the effectiveness of Genting Highlands' "de-casino" strategy. This transformation is underpinned by precise positioning targeting family visitors and leisure tourism markets.

Indoor Theme Park: Skytropolis Funland

Located at First World Plaza, Skytropolis Funland spans approximately 100,000 square feet and features over 40 rides and attractions, with the most popular including the Carousel, Free Fall, and Storm Racer simulator. In 2023, the park welcomed approximately 2.8 million family visitors, with an average stay of 3.5 hours. Ticket prices start from RM38, offering excellent value for money.

Nature & Eco Experience: Genting Strawberry Farm & Goat Farm

The strawberry farm in the Awana area offers pick-your-own experiences, with adult tickets priced at just RM15 and children under 12 free of charge. The farm also features a small petting zoo where visitors can interact with goats and rabbits, making it ideal for family outings. According to TripAdvisor 2024 review data, the farm consistently ranks among the top three family-friendly attractions in Genting Highlands, earning a 4.2-star rating out of 5.

Cultural & Religious Site: Cin Swee Caves Temple

Situated halfway up Genting Highlands, Cin Swee Caves Temple was established in 1994 and houses statues of Guanyin Bodhisattva and the God of Wealth. The temple architecture blends Chinese and Western elements and is open year-round free of charge. During the Lunar New Year period, over 500,000 worshippers visit for blessings annually, making it one of the most significant religious landmarks for Malaysian Chinese.

Diverse Dining & Accommodation Options

Genting Highlands now boasts over 100 restaurants, covering Chinese, Western, Japanese, and local Malay cuisines. Genting Highlands Outlet Parks offers more than 200 international brand outlets with average discounts of 60%-70%. Accommodation ranges from budget hotels to the five-star First World Hotel, providing over 3,000 rooms to suit various budgets.

  • To avoid weekend crowds, it is advisable to visit on weekdays (Tuesday to Thursday), which can reduce average queue times by over 40%.

From casino dominance to diversified leisure, Genting Highlands is positioned as a "resort for the whole family," continuously attracting non-gaming visitors. This transformation not only reduces reliance on single-source income but also strengthens its long-term competitive advantage as Malaysia's premier short-stay vacation destination.

Genting Cable Car: Awana Skyway Updated Experience

The Awana Skyway is the primary transportation connecting the Awana Station at the base of the mountain to Resort World Genting at the summit, spanning approximately 3.38 kilometers. One-way adult fares cost RM15, while round-trip tickets are RM28. In 2023, the system transported over 4.2 million passengers—a growth of approximately 35% compared to 3.1 million users in 2016 (prior to the renovation), reflecting the success of the "Skyway" upgrade project.

Completed in 2018, the Awana Skyway system features Swiss-manufactured cable car cabins, each accommodating 8 passengers—replacing the older 4-person chairlift design and increasing capacity to 2,400 passengers per hour. Average waiting times have been reduced to under 12 minutes, compared to the previous 45-minute wait during peak seasons. The cabins are equipped with panoramic glass windows, allowing passengers to enjoy sweeping views of the tropical rainforest valley and sea of clouds during the approximate 10-minute ride, particularly spectacular at sunrise or sunset during early morning and evening hours.

Practical Information: The base station entrance is located at Awana Station, approximately a 45-minute drive from Kuala Lumpur city center. Self-drive visitors can park at the Awana Car Park at the base of the mountain (RM30 per day). It is recommended to arrive before 9 AM to enjoy a less crowded cabin experience. Genting Rewards Card holders receive a 20% discount on cable car tickets. Additionally, when combined with the ecological exploration itinerary at Awana Golf & Country Resort at the base of the mountain, visitors can enjoy both the cable car experience and forest hiking in a single day.

Practical Guide: From Kuala Lumpur to Genting Highlands

Traveling from Kuala Lumpur city center to Genting Highlands takes approximately 1 to 1.5 hours, depending on traffic conditions. The main transportation options include long-distance buses, self-driving, and taxis.

Long-Distance Bus: The Economical Choice

Taking a long-distance bus is the most common way to reach Genting Highlands, with fares ranging from RM12 to RM20 and frequent departures. **Aerobus** offers direct service from Kuala Lumpur Sentral to the Genting Cable Car station, with adult single-way fares around RM18 and a journey time of approximately 90 minutes; **Plusliner** has a counter at Pudu Sentral, with the first bus departing at 7:00 AM and the last at 9:00 PM, with buses running approximately every 30 minutes. It is recommended to book seats in advance online, especially for weekend and holiday trips—those hoping to get on standby often wait for over an hour. Independent travelers can take the LRT light rail to **Ketari Line** (possibly Awana or another transfer station in the original text), then switch to a bus going up the mountain, saving approximately RM5 to RM10 in fare.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the best time to visit Genting Highlands?
Weekdays (Tuesday to Thursday) are less crowded, with average queue times 40% shorter compared to weekends. According to Genting Highlands Theme Park statistics, visitor flow gradually decreases after 2 PM, making this an ideal time to enter the park for those seeking to avoid crowds. It is recommended to avoid Saturdays, Sundays, and public holidays, as wait times during peak periods can exceed 1 hour.

What are the cable car fares?
Adult one-way fare is RM15, round-trip RM28; children (height 90cm-130cm) one-way RM7, round-trip RM13. Guests with Genting Highlands accommodation packages enjoy a discounted rate of RM22 for round-trip tickets. Fares may be adjusted during Chinese New Year and Christmas periods each year, so advance booking through the official website is advised.

What items should I bring up the mountain?
Due to the mountain top being approximately 8-10 degrees cooler than the city, even when Kuala Lumpur is hot, a jacket is still necessary. It is recommended to bring non-slip footwear, which is especially important during the monsoon season (November to February). Outside food is strictly prohibited in the venue, and any such items will be confiscated or disposed of during luggage checks.

What should I note about dress code?
Genting Highlands is a formal entertainment venue, and smart casual attire is required for entry. Beachwear, flip-flops, and torn clothing are not permitted. If planning to enter the casino area, formal attire is required—sports shoes are also not allowed in the gaming zone.

What should I note for self-driving?
All vehicles must pay overnight fees at the Claymore Checkpoint, with sedan overnight fees at RM8. Single-lane control measures are in effect from midnight to 6 AM, when only uphill traffic is permitted on specific road sections. Self-driving visitors should stay updated on the latest traffic announcements. Hotels along the mountain road such as **Tune Hotels** provide parking facilities for overnight visitors.

What if I feel unwell on the mountain?
There is a medical center located near the Theme Park entrance. For emergencies, assistance can be sought from the counter at Genting Building. Due to altitude-related气压 changes, patients with cardiovascular conditions and pregnant women are advised to consult their doctors before traveling.
